---
title: "Inference margins are quietly compressing"
date: "2026-03-12"
tags: ["infrastructure", "economics"]
---

Per-token prices keep falling, but the more interesting story is on the cost side: model providers are now competing on inference efficiency as aggressively as on capability. The gap between published list prices and the marginal cost of a token is narrowing fast.

For agent builders, this means two things. First, "long-running, tool-heavy" workloads — once a budget red flag — are becoming defensible. Second, providers' strategic moat shifts from "we have the best model" to "we have the best stack underneath the best model." Hardware, scheduling, and KV-cache reuse start to matter more than benchmark scores.
