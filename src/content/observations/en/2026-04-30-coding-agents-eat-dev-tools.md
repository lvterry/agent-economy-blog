---
title: "Coding agents are eating the dev tools market"
date: "2026-04-30"
tags: ["agents", "dev-tools"]
---

The line between IDE, code-review tool, and CI is collapsing. What used to be three discrete products is increasingly a single agent loop that reads a repo, proposes a change, runs the tests, and opens the PR. The implication isn't that one of these categories wins — it's that the shape of "developer tooling" itself shifts from feature-rich UIs to a small number of well-orchestrated agents with deep repo context.

Watch which incumbents reframe themselves around agent surfaces vs. defending feature parity. The former group will compound; the latter will be commoditized.
