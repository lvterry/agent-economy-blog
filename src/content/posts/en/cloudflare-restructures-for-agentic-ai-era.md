May 8, 2026 — Cloudflare co-founders Matthew Prince and Michelle Zatlyn sent a company-wide memo announcing the reduction of more than 1,100 positions globally, roughly 10% of the workforce.

This is not a cost-cutting round. The memo explicitly frames the layoffs as a strategic reorganization for the "agentic AI era." Cloudflare's internal AI usage has increased by 600% in the last quarter alone. Employees across engineering, HR, finance, and marketing now run thousands of AI agent sessions every day to complete their work.

"We are reimagining every internal process, team, and role across the company," the founders wrote. "Today's actions are about Cloudflare defining how a world-class, high-growth company operates and creates value in the agentic AI era."

Departing employees receive full base pay through end of 2026, healthcare through year-end, and accelerated equity vesting through August 15. The company described the approach as deliberate and intended to avoid drawn-out, multi-quarter reductions.

For an infrastructure giant like Cloudflare to publicly anchor a major workforce restructuring on AI agent adoption is a striking signal. It suggests that automation is not a theoretical future — it is already reshaping how leading tech companies operate, at a pace that surprised even insiders.
