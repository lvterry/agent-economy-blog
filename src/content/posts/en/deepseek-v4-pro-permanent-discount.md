DeepSeek has converted its 75% promotional discount on V4 Pro API pricing into a permanent price reduction, a move that reshapes the competitive dynamics of the AI inference market.

The permanent pricing for DeepSeek V4 Pro — which boasts a 1M context window and 384K max output tokens — now stands at:
- Cache-hit input: $0.003625/M tokens
- Cache-miss input: $0.435/M tokens  
- Output: $0.87/M tokens

This is one-quarter of the original launch price, effective after the promotional period ends on May 31. The V4 Flash model remains even more aggressive at $0.28/M output tokens.

The strategic logic here goes beyond price cuts. DeepSeek is using permanent low pricing to lock in developer mindshare. When inference costs compress this far, the differentiation shifts from "who is cheaper" to "who integrates better into the toolchain." DeepSeek already supports Claude Code, OpenAgents, and multiple agent frameworks alongside its OpenAI-compatible and Anthropic-format APIs.

This permanent price shift may matter more than any model launch. It lowers the operating bar for every application-layer developer building on LLMs, and puts pressure on every other provider to justify higher pricing with genuinely differentiated capabilities.
